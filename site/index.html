📋 Book in this order
  1. Passports first — all 3 (child too) valid 6+ months past Feb 2027 (into Aug 2027) with 2+ blank pages each.
  2. The big ticket — open-jaw (BOS→DAC + DPS→BOS) or the Turkish stopover version — on the airline's US website/app. Booking there starts the federal 24-hour free-cancel window and keeps refunds simple. Avoid third-party sites.
  3. Same day: Bali resort on Marriott points (5 nights, 5th free). Points rates usually cancel free until a few days out — if the hotel doesn't work, cancel the flight within 24h at $0.
  4. Bangladesh visas — see Visas below. Do this before buying the middle leg.
  5. Dhaka→Bali hop last (THAI / AirAsia). It never touches the US so the 24-hour rule does not apply — treat it as non-refundable and buy it only once everything else is locked.
  6. ~1 week before Bali: Indonesia e-VOA online for all 3 (needs your DPS→BOS booking as onward proof) + Bali tourist levy + the customs form ≤3 days before landing.
💳 Cancellations & refunds
  • 24-hour rule (US DOT): any ticket that touches the US — the open-jaw, the stopover itinerary, or BOS→DAC / DPS→BOS one-ways — is fully refundable for 24 hours after booking if departure is 7+ days away. This is the main safety valve; Turkish honors it only when booked via its own site/app.
  • After 24h on Turkish: economy cancel fee roughly $70–200+ per person; the cheapest "EcoFly" promo fares are non-refundable. If unsure, pay the small step-up to a flexible economy fare — the exact rule shows at checkout. No cancellations in the last 12h before departure.
  • Dhaka→Bali: no US protection. AirAsia is effectively non-refundable; THAI charges fare-rule fees. Assume this money is spent once booked.
  • Airline-initiated changes: if the airline significantly changes or cancels any flight, you're owed a full refund regardless of fare type.
  • Card refunds must arrive within 7 business days (DOT). Pay with a credit card that has trip protection.
  • Chase UR points: portal bookings get real Turkish (235-) ticket numbers, so the stopover hotel usually still works — but N/R fare classes are excluded, and changes route through Chase. Cleanest: book the airline direct with the card, spend UR on the THAI leg instead.
🛂 Visas & borders
  • Bangladesh: US passports get a 30-day visa on arrival at Dhaka — $50 cash USD per person (child too; carry $150 crisp), onward ticket + proof of funds (~$500), and it's granted at the officer's discretion — getting tourist visas in advance from the mission is the safer play. Your stay keeps a buffer: never let the Feb 1–3 departure slip past day 30.
  • Indonesia: e-VOA (B1), $35/person, 30 days; apply online ≥48h before arrival; requires onward-flight proof (your DPS→BOS leg); extendable only in person now. Plus the Bali levy (IDR 150k/person, pay online) and the electronic customs declaration ≤3 days before (QR scanned at the airport).
  • Istanbul layover/stopover: US citizens are visa-free in Türkiye (90 days/180) — leaving the airport with the baby is fine, no visa needed.
  • Transit (KL / Bangkok / Doha / Seoul): on a single ticket you stay airside — no visa. Only if you ever book the KL hop as two separate tickets would you clear Malaysian immigration (visa-free for US, but file the free Malaysia Digital Arrival Card ≤3 days before).
😴 Long-rest layovers (12h+ with the baby)
  • Qatar via Doha (easiest): when booking the open-jaw, pick the itinerary with the 17 hr overnight in Doha (~$100 over the cheapest option). Land ~5pm, sleep in a real bed, fly on ~10:30am. Discover Qatar stopover hotels run from ~$14/person (4-star) booked with the ticket; US passports enter Qatar visa-free.
  • Turkish via Istanbul: IST→DAC flies once daily, so the standard connection is ~6 hr — the longer option is the tracked ~30 hr stopover itinerary (see Today tab). Turkish gives economy passengers a FREE 4-star hotel night when the connection is 20 hr–7 days on one reservation: book it, then apply at their Stopover "Booker" page ≥72 hr before the flight. Not valid on N/R fare classes — check the booking-class letter before paying.
